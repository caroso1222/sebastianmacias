I quitted my Job, Now What?

I could say, with some certainty, I have had almost everything I wanted in my life, but now is moment to stretch me outside my comfort zone.

I have grown in a medium class family in Colombia, South America. My father grew up in Cali and Armenia, two cities where the Salsa music and the coffee culture are very strong. My mother comes from Caldas, one of the regions where the people are called “Paisas” and the entrepreneur spirit is well known. My parents met each other in Bogotá, in the capital of my country. This mix of cultures has created the man I am today, an extroverted, curios and hardworking guy, with opened mind and a world that cannot ends in the borders of my city.

I studied the engineering I wanted, after a quickly shift in my first semester, and I did it in one of the top universities in Latin America. I have worked in international robotics projects with NASA and the European Space Foundation, and these positions gave me the possibility to travel around the world. I had the unique opportunity to work with excellent engineers, designers, teachers and experts, and I learned about the importance of working as a team. I was enjoying each moment, I was happy.

I had the opportunity to learn about science, art, design and others engineerings thanks to the flexibility of my university. I took a pottery class in the same semester I was doing my thesis, it was one of the craziest thing I did that as engineer, but there I met a wonderful, crazy and out of this world people. I traveled around the USA with two ladies with the biggest hearts I ever met, and I was forced to be vegan, or that was they tried. I have met different cultures and people from India, Bangladesh, Canada, Austria, Australia, Slovenia, Brazil and the list continues. The thing is I had the possibility to be around different thinking ways and it made me a better person. I was in continuous learning, I was excited.

Then, I have gone across the "just graduated" status, critical moment in your life when your first employ probably will define your next 5 years salary and jobs. No company hires you because you have not experience, or your salary is just and insult, but it is what you have to do to climb up in the working world. I had an amazing opportunity working in a well-known ex-girlfriend’s father’s company, an overwhelming but secure job for long period of time. After a tortuous way, I learned what I had to learn there, and I quitted in the moment when I finally understood that I was in the wrong place, but that is another post. In this part of my life I was disappointed and feeling lost.

The thing is I just knew I was in the wrong place, but actually already I don’t know where is the place I have to be. I see pictures of my friends in Facebook, Instagram and Twitter about what fantastic are their lifes in big companies, just waiting for the weekend and vacations to do whatever they like, but it is not for me. My parents says I have to work in a huge company and last years there, even if this is not what I like, but, that’s what succeed means for my father’s generations.

I have decided to do whatever I want and I will do it now, even if this imply going against my family’s thinking, my friends’ mindset and the people’s advices.

I know I have to cross the border of my comfort zone, I know it won’t be easy but I want to be a global entrepreneur.

This is my first post in english ever, and this is a different way to practice this language and tell about this new journey. If you think I could improve something just comment.
